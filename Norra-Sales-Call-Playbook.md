# Norra — Sales Call Playbook

Your script for turning a demo into a "yes." Keep it conversational — these are guide rails,
not a teleprompter. Goal of every first call: **book the free 2-week pilot.**

---

## Before the call (2 min prep)
- Open their **website** and pre-load it in the **Demo Console** so the demo is instant.
- Know their type (clinic, salon, trades) so your examples land.

## 1. Opening (30 seconds)
> "Hi [Name], I'm Sashi — I run Norra, a local AI receptionist for businesses like yours.
> Quick question before I take any of your time: when your front desk is busy or you're
> closed, roughly how many calls go to voicemail?"

Let them answer. Then:
> "Right — and most of those never call back. I built something that answers every one of
> those, books the appointment, and texts you a summary. Can I show you yours working in
> about two minutes?"

## 2. Discovery (ask 2–3, listen)
- How many calls a day, roughly? Who covers the phone when it's busy or after hours?
- Do calls get missed at lunch / evenings / weekends?
- What's a new patient/customer worth to you over a year?

(This makes the cost of missed calls *their* number, not yours.)

## 3. The demo (the wow — let it do the talking)
> "What's your website? … okay, give me 15 seconds … now call this number and ask it
> anything — your hours, your prices, book an appointment."

Hand them the demo number. Let them talk to *their own* receptionist. Then:
> "That just answered as your clinic, in your voice, and it would've booked that into your
> calendar. It does that 24/7, in your patients' languages."

## 4. Pricing (say it with confidence, no flinching)
> "Plans start at **$149/month**. Most clinics are on **Pro at $297** — that's less than a
> fraction of a part-time receptionist, and I set the whole thing up and support it. There's
> a one-time setup, no long-term contract."

Then go straight to the pilot offer (don't wait for them to react to price):
> "But here's what I'd suggest — let me set it up **free for two weeks** so you can see it
> book real appointments. If it earns its keep, we continue. If not, no cost."

## 5. Objection handling
- **"How much is it?"** → "Less than a fraction of hiring — and unlike a hire, it never calls in sick or misses a call. Most owners make it back in a couple of recovered bookings."
- **"Is it really AI? Will callers know?"** → "It sounds remarkably human — but it's honest: it introduces itself as a virtual assistant. Callers are happy because they get instant answers and booked in, 24/7."
- **"Will it replace my receptionist?"** → "It's there to back them up — it catches the overflow, after-hours, and missed calls so your team isn't chained to the phone. You decide how much it handles."
- **"What about my current phone system?"** → "You keep your number. We just forward it to Norra when you can't pick up — nothing changes for your callers."
- **"Is my data safe?"** → "Yes — it's privacy-conscious and PHIPA-aware for clinics, recorded with consent, only collecting what's needed to help the caller."
- **"I need to think about it."** → "Totally fair — that's exactly why the trial is free for two weeks. Zero risk, I do all the setup. Worst case you've lost nothing; best case it's booking you extra appointments by next week."

## 6. The close
> "Let's get your free trial started this week. What calendar or booking system do you use —
> Jane, Google? I'll connect it and have Norra live in a couple of days."

Get a concrete next step: the booking system + a go-live day.

## 7. After the call
- Send the **leave-behind one-pager** (PDF) + a short follow-up email (in the pilot kit).
- Add them to a simple tracker (name, type, status, next step).
- After the pilot: ask for the **numbers** and a **testimonial** → put it on norrahq.com.
